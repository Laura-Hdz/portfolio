150 Canada

2014, digital (Illustrator), print

Canada 150 is a fun logo created for the celebration of Canada’s 150th anniversary as part of a Dawson College project.

It combines two of the most well known Canadian symbols—the maple leaf and the moose—into a simple, organic shape. The old-style numerals point towards the (old) age of the country while assimilating itself into the logo through the type’s roundness. The typography clarifies the purpose of the logo, but is not necessary in order for the viewer to recognize what it is representing. The red as the main colour was an obvious choice, particularly for its reference to the Canadian flag. The logo was meant to be flexible, both in colour and application.
